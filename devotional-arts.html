🌸 Youth & Kids Programs

At Sribas Angan Bhakti Vriksha, we nurture spirituality in children through engaging and joyful experiences. Our Youth & Kids Programs are specially designed for young minds to absorb timeless values in a fun and creative way. Children learn through interactive storytelling from the Ramayana and Mahabharata, chanting slokas, devotional drawing and coloring, and cultural arts like classical dance. These sessions spark curiosity about Krishna consciousness while building compassion, discipline, and devotion from a young age.

🌱 Let your child’s heart grow in the garden of devotion.

Bhajan Singing

"Chant and be happy" — this was Srila Prabhupada’s loving instruction to the world. Bhajan singing is not merely a musical art, but a heartfelt expression of devotion to Lord Krishna. In our training, youths learn to sing traditional Vaishnava bhajans written by great saints like Narottama Dasa Thakura, Bhaktivinoda Thakura, and Srila Prabhupada. Emphasis is placed on clear pronunciation, mood, and the meditative power of the holy names. Through regular practice, students develop a deep connection with the divine and cultivate inner peace and joy. Singing becomes seva — a spiritual offering that purifies the heart and inspires others on their devotional path.

Mridanga

Master this traditional percussion instrument used in kirtans. Youths are trained in rhythm and devotion to accompany sankirtan performancesThe mridanga drum is the heartbeat of Harinama Sankirtana. It is said that just by hearing the mridanga's rhythmic beat, one's sins are washed away and the soul is awakened. Our training introduces youth to the traditional Bengali mridanga style used in ISKCON kirtans, beginning with foundational bols (rhythmic syllables) and progressing to dynamic kirtan playing. Students are also taught how to accompany bhajans and sankirtan groups in a coordinated and spiritually uplifting manner. This is not just drumming — it’s an act of glorification, a sacred sound that pleases the Lord and spreads His mercy everywhere.

×

Harmonium

The harmonium is the melodic soul of devotional music in ISKCON. In our harmonium training sessions, youth learn how to play Vaishnava bhajans, accompany kirtans, and support group singing. Special focus is given to raga structure, hand positioning, and correct breath-like phrasing, making the harmonium an extension of one’s devotion. Many of our teachers were trained by senior ISKCON devotees and bring a spiritual perspective to the art — that music is meant to glorify Krishna and touch hearts. Playing harmonium becomes a sadhana — a devotional practice that purifies the consciousness.

Devotional Dance

Dance has always been a divine medium to express love for the Lord — from the Gopis' Ras Lila to Chaitanya Mahaprabhu’s ecstatic kirtans. Our devotional dance training guides young participants to offer their movements as seva to Krishna. From graceful Bharatanatyam-inspired gestures to expressive Vaishnava storytelling through steps, our students learn to embody spiritual themes such as Krishna’s pastimes, Rama’s victories, or Radha’s love. Dance becomes a living expression of shastra, art, and heart. It builds confidence, discipline, and most importantly — joy in spiritual life.
